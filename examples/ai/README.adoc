= AI Pieces

A small suite of Sound Garden programs generated by LLM. Play any of them with:

----
$ play_program < examples/ai/garden_bells.txt
----

or render to a file (with level statistics):

----
$ render_program 30 out.wav --stats < examples/ai/garden_bells.txt
----

The pieces are annotated with dropped quotations like `[ notes with spaces ] drop`
and every one ends with a `limit` safety stage. Pieces that use randomness pin a `seed:<N>` so renders
are reproducible — delete the seed word for a fresh take every run.

== garden_bells.txt

Six-voice polyphonic bells. A Euclidean `x(5,8)` trigger pattern fires notes
from a hexatonic arpeggio (with an alternating `<A4;F#4>` final step) into a
`poly:6` quotation: each voice is a sine plus a quiet third harmonic, shaped
by an exponential impulse. A dotted-eighth saturating delay (`fbsat`) smears
the bells into a wash, a five-second `verb` blooms behind them, a slow sine
pans the result and `width` opens the field a little past natural.

== tide.txt

A drone. Three detuned band-limited saws (`saw` is polyBLEP now — no aliasing
in the undertow) are summed and pushed through a biquad low-pass whose cutoff
sweeps very slowly (0.05 Hz) between 180 and 1500 Hz, then through a breathing
`notch` (300–900 Hz) for phaser-like movement. `drive` rounds off filter
overshoot with gain-compensated saturation, a slow sine pans the result, and
the wet half of the mix is an eight-second `verb` stretched wide with `width`.

== rain.txt

Stochastic resonant pings. A random trigger pattern (`x|.x|..|x.`) is thinned
by `chance` (three drops in four survive), then each surviving drop both fires
a short noise impulse and draws a fresh resonant frequency from `rnd`
(400–5000 Hz, exponential). Two cascaded narrow band-passes (`bp`, Q 20) turn
each impulse into a damped droplet; saturating feedback adds distant echoes
and a short dark `verb` wets the streets. Seeded — remove `seed:11` for
different rain each run.

== pendulum.txt

FM bass line. A four-step pattern (with `<E2;G2>` alternation) sets the
carrier; `lag` glides between the MIDI notes (linear in semitones) before
`m2f`, so the bass slides instead of stepping. A 2.01-ratio modulator gives
slightly inharmonic sidebands and the same ADSR scales both the modulation
index and the output, so notes open up spectrally as they swell. `drive` adds
growl, a long `fbsat` dub echo trails the phrase ends, and `comp` glues the
line together.

== drum_circle.txt

Minimal kit. The kick is a sine whose pitch is the trigger impulse raised to
the third power (a fast 162→42 Hz sweep). The snare is a band-passed noise
burst at 1800 Hz with a flash of small-room `verb` blended behind it. The hat
is high-passed noise on an off-beat pattern with a random ghost subdivision
(`x|[.x]`) and an alternating double-hit, then `crush` (5 bits, 9 kHz) grinds
it to grit. A second ghost-hat line runs every off-eighth but `chance` lets
only three in ten through. `comp` glues the bus. Seeded.

== night_walk.txt

A melody that finds its own way. Each step of a sixteen-cell trigger pattern
makes `rnd` draw a new pitch from a two-octave span and `scale:dorian` snaps
it into key. The lead is a `poly:4` quotation — a band-limited triangle with
a whisper of octave — so every tail keeps ringing on its own pitch while the
melody moves on. Underneath, a mono shadow bass follows the same notes an
octave down through `lag2` — quick on the way up, reluctant on the way down.
A high `poly:2` sine answers only when `chance` (40%) allows it. When tails
pile up, `comp` leans on them softly; then saturated echo and a six-second
`verb` widened past the walls for empty streets at 3am. Seeded — remove
`seed:7` to wander anew.

== rust.txt

Machines dreaming of the sea. The engine is a wavefolded saw bass (`fold`
amount breathing with a slow sine) gated by an ADSR pattern. Above it a
siren: a slow LFO quantized to a diminished arpeggio by `deg:0,3,6,9`, with
`lag` melting the steps back into a slide. Percussion is noise crushed to
3 bits at 4 kHz. The whole bus goes through `drive`, saturating feedback,
a factory-hall `verb` opened with `width`, and a 4:1 `comp`. Seeded.

== ghost_choir.txt

Call and answer with the dead. Every 4.5 seconds a three-partial chord
(root, fifth, octave; the root alternates C/Eb/G/F each cycle) swells for a
second and dies. At the moment it dies, `spectral_freeze` captures its
spectrum and sustains it through the three silent cells — the ghost — while
`spectral_shuffle:12` (re-drawn once per cycle) lets the ghost's partials
drift to neighbouring frequencies. A second ADSR makes the ghost bloom and
fade to nothing before the next chord. The ghost is stretched wider than the
living voice with `width`, the living voice is mixed in front about 8 dB
above, and a stone-nave `verb` holds them both. Seeded.
